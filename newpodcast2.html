Hi.

Thanks for checking out my new audio series interviewing "professional makers", those that make a living through small, independent, manufacturing.

I love this topic.
Its seems so primal and natural to me: make useful things for yourself and others.
How you are compensated may have changed, but the spirit has stayed much the same.
Its exciting times in the 21st century. Being able to have better and cheaper tools than ever before, to enable more people to produce physical products.
It's so intersting for me to hear the stories of inspiration, failure, and success of these individuals who rely on us, the buyers of these physical goods, for their livelihood.

My first guest, Mikey Sklar, tackles these subjects. His electronic-kit business is aligned with his self-suffieciency goals, and enables otheres to do so as well.
It didn't start out that way. But since his early days of making "electric clothing", Mikey has learned a thing or two about what it takes to produce and market his kits.
